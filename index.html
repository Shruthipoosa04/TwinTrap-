💡 About: Fake WiFi Detection & Security Awareness

🔴 What is an Evil Twin Attack? +
An Evil Twin Attack is a cyber attack where a hacker creates a fake WiFi network that appears identical to a legitimate one. When users connect, the attacker can intercept their data, steal login credentials, and inject malware.
⚠ Why Detecting Fake WiFi is Important? +
Fake WiFi networks allow hackers to perform Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) attacks and steal sensitive data.

Risks include:
  • Identity theft and credential theft
  • Financial fraud via stolen banking information
  • Malware injection and device compromise
  • Corporate espionage and data leaks
🛑 What are Common WiFi Vulnerabilities & Attacks? +
WiFi networks are vulnerable to several types of cyber attacks:
  • MitM Attack: Intercepting communication between users and websites.
  • Packet Sniffing: Capturing unencrypted data on open networks.
  • DoS: Disrupting connectivity by overwhelming access points.
  • WiFi Phishing: Fake login portals used to steal credentials.
  • KRACK Attack: Exploiting WPA2 encryption weaknesses.
